About

Most coaches handle either the emotional side of a transition or the strategic side. Rarely both.

My background spans strategy, private equity, data analytics, and consulting at senior levels. But I also left that world deliberately, on my own terms. That combination is rare: the frameworks of a strategist applied to questions of identity, purpose, and what actually matters.

I do not deal in fluff. I treat a transition the way a good strategist would — with a clear-eyed assessment of where things stand, a stress-tested financial picture, and a concrete roadmap for what follows.

I work with senior professionals who have built impressive careers and are now facing a question that career growth alone cannot answer.

What I Do

Coaching for the moments
that define what comes next.

01

Transitions out of the corporate world

The decision to leave a successful corporate career is among the most complex a professional will face. Financial confidence, identity, purpose, and plan: we work through all of it.

02

Transitions into leadership positions

A new leadership role does not just change the title. It changes what is required. We build the habits, decisions, and presence that make the transition stick.

03

Business scale-ups for CEOs and business heads

Growth creates new problems. We work on the strategy, the team, and the mindset of the person at the top, so that scaling the business does not mean losing yourself in it.

04

Career inflections

At the critical junctures, the wrong choice compounds over years. We think it through together: the offer, the pivot, the role that does not feel right, the opportunity that might change everything.

05

Complex problem-solving, decision-making and implementation

Some challenges do not fit a category. High stakes, unclear options, competing pressures. That is precisely where rigour matters most.
